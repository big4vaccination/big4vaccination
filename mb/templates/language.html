Overview

Australia's multicultural society is made up of migrants who were born in each country over the world. Cultural diversity has always been a part of Australiaâ€™s history. The society is continuing to become culturally diverse over time.
With a growing trend of new community that settled in Australia, the nation acquires various new cultural assets, with new cultures, customs, festivals and changes for a greater understanding of the world.

The Australian Government state that each Australian has the right to engage and communicate with the Australian Government and fundamental service regardless of their English ability, their language preference and culture or linguistic background.

According to the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS) survey results, around 660 000 people who were born overseas self-reported as having language barriers in English.

The Australian Government provides language services for helping people who have language barriers in English can access the essential services and programs they need in Australian multicultural society.

Language services can include:

  • engaging a credentialed interpreter either in person, via telephone or video conference
  • translation of government documents from English into community languages
  • translation of personal documents from a language other than English
  • assistance from a bilingual staff member (who has received appropriate training)
  • information on websites translated into community languages
  • multilingual telephone information
  • multimedia resources and other digital media in languages other than English
